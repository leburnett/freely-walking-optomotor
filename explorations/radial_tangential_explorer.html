Radial & Tangential Velocity Explorer

In a circular arena, any velocity can be decomposed into two perpendicular components relative to the arena center:
Radial velocity (vrad) = how fast the dot moves toward/away from the center. Positive = outward (away from center). Negative = inward (centring).
Tangential velocity (vtan) = how fast the dot moves around the center (orbiting). Positive = counter-clockwise.
ωarena = vtan / r — arena-centric angular velocity: how fast the dot's position angle changes relative to the arena center. Depends on distance from center.
Turning rate (dθheading/dt) — fly-centric angular velocity: how fast the dot's heading direction is rotating (= path curvature × speed). This is what you measure from fly tracking data and what the optomotor response directly drives.

Scenario 1 shows that a perfectly circular path centered on the arena produces zero radial velocity — all movement is tangential. But offsetting the circle from center creates oscillating radial velocity, even though the path shape hasn't changed.
Scenario 2 shows that an asymmetric (elliptical) path produces oscillating radial velocity even when centered on the arena, because the dot's distance from center varies as it traverses the ellipse.
